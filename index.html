Why This Signal Matters

PATTERN RECOGNITION,
APPLIED DIFFERENTLY

Most people who interview founders are journalists or fans. Natalie spent years doing something else entirely: evaluating C level executives across industries, assessing how leaders think, how they build teams, and what separates the ones who scale from the ones who stall. Hundreds of conversations. Boardrooms, not podcasts.

Built Young takes that same pattern recognition and points it at a different group: founders under 25 who are already generating real revenue without anyone handing them a playbook. The questions are sharper because they come from someone trained to find signal in how people talk about what they have built, not just what they have built.

For investors, this is an early warning system. For founders, it is proof that someone serious is paying attention. For anyone watching, it is a front row seat to the operators who will define the next decade of business, captured before the narrative gets polished.
